⑤ Recently I’ve spent significant time thinking about my academic history, especially Zhongkao and ACT Prep. Because I encounter a lot of problems under the quite intense life, the best way I can figure out how to solve them is to look back and see how I tried to solve similar problems in the past. In addition, by constantly thinking about the best periods of my personal academic history—2014 and 2016—and regarding Me from the Past as my role model, I’m also building up much greater confidence and motivation.

⑥ Thanks to my habit of recording my studying modes in the past 5 years! It helps me put my current state into perspective and predict my future performance. The history of the People’s Republic is also a vital source of inspiration.

⑦ Indeed, the more I learn and better myself and gain more opportunities, the more problems I will encounter. Similar principles apply to the society as well, as the growing complexity of our society requires more skilled people. And, the more difficult a person’s job is, the more mistakes he will make because of the complexity of the situation.

Thanks again Diary! Looking back at my old self, I know that many fundamental qualities only gets reinforced over time; however, I also had a lot of weaknesses.

These comparisons helped me greatly in evaluating myself. Hopefully one day I can fully elaborate my thoughts because now I only have these vague feelings.

⑩ The speed at which my weaknesses are eliminated is so high that I often feel a sense of discomfort, as I need time for the new quality to sink in. Recall how often I’ve felt this way after the revolution of 5/0 Incident?

⑬ Occasionally I would read some articles that talk about learning is becoming “useless” because knowledge is updating rapidly or AI are taking over many jobs. I doubt whether these essays are written “intentionally by the ruling class so as to create anxiety among the populace and stall their motivation to learn.”

⑭ From my recent experience, the situation in which the things I learned get almost immediate use in life, whether in social interactions, or solving problems, or in helping me learning other related knowledge, or shape quality and confidence in general—has happened a huge number of times. I can’t find words to describe the benefits of studying.

⑮ My studying system has grown increasingly sophisticated, with so many components that need to be coordinated. Because there are so many of them—social life, health, direction, motivation, attitude, time management, environment, materials—that I can never achieve optimized states in all of these aspects, but I’m still striving to improve.

⑯ I’m also increasingly appreciating the huge number of ways of effective learning. There are so many methods that using one method at a time for one course is enough. Thinking from the perspective of teachers, visualization, talking with other people… the list goes on and on.

⑰ In LinAlg classes I continue to pay full attention without making much notes; in Stats and DL, however, I can only hastily copy what the teacher writes and then try to understand my notes afterwards. This is a “second-best” tactic, as I had planned to not take notes in classes as an ideal method.

⑱ Sometimes I would again enter the state of rapid knowledge integration, but unlike in January, I no longer feel intense excitement when this happens. My prediction came true! Indeed, there is no reason to feel complacent because everyone knows some valuable things that I don’t know, and it’s crucial that I keep humble and acknowledge how less I know. Time continue to pass slowly as there are so many things that can be done in a day.

Now, on the other hand, I and other dashens are naturally attracted to each other, and I simply ignore those who don’t share with me the same values in favor of being alone, thus I feel Vandy is better than NFLS.
